The Journey of Our Energy-Efficient System: From Prototype to Future Developments

Introduction

In our pursuit of creating an innovative energy-saving system, we embarked on a journey that began with a well-planned prototype and evolved through several stages of development. This blog details the process, highlighting key decisions and advancements that have shaped our project.

Initial Prototype: Laying the Groundwork

Sensor Selection

The first critical decision in developing our prototype was the choice of sensor. We opted for a standard ultrasonic sensor due to its ease of use, affordability, and high accuracy. Ultrasonic sensors are known for their reliability and efficiency, making them an excellent choice for our initial testing. Their ability to accurately measure distances while being cost-effective was crucial in allowing us to test our ideas without significant financial investment.

For more information, visit Ultrasonic Sensor Info.

Microcontroller Choice

For the microcontroller, we selected the Arduino UNO. This decision was based on the Arduino UNO’s user-friendly coding environment, availability, and its ability to meet our current requirements. Its straightforward programming interface and widespread community support made it a practical choice for prototyping. The Arduino UNO allowed us to effectively manage the sensor data and control various components, ensuring that we could test and refine our system efficiently.

Circuit Board Design

In our prototype, we used a standard breadboard. The decision to use a breadboard rather than soldering components onto a permanent circuit board was driven by the need for flexibility during the prototyping phase. A breadboard allowed us to easily modify and adjust our circuit design without the risk of permanent changes. This approach facilitated quick testing and iteration, helping us identify and resolve issues before committing to a final design.

System Functionality

The primary function of our prototype was to count the number of entries and exits in a room. To achieve this, the ultrasonic sensors were strategically placed at doorways. The Arduino UNO processed the sensor data and used it to toggle lights and fans based on the number of people detected. The system utilized high and low signals to control these components, providing a basic but effective demonstration of the concept.

Transition to Future Developments

Advanced Sensor Selection

For the next phase, we chose to integrate PIR (Passive Infrared) and mmWave (millimeter-wave) sensors. The mmWave sensors use radio waves to measure distances, offering higher accuracy and the ability to detect motion without requiring a direct line of sight. However, they are prone to false positives, which can affect their reliability. On the other hand, PIR sensors, while less accurate and requiring a line of sight, have very low false positive rates. By combining these two technologies, we aim to achieve a high level of accuracy in detecting movement and occupancy, leveraging the strengths of each sensor type to minimize their individual limitations.

For more information, visit PIR Sensor Info and mmWave Sensor Info.

Microcontroller Upgrade

To manage the increased complexity of our system, we selected the Raspberry Pi 3 B as our microcontroller. The Raspberry Pi 3 B is capable of handling a large number of modules and communicating with various microcontrollers and servers. Its advanced processing power and versatility make it well-suited for managing the more sophisticated requirements of our enhanced system. Additionally, we plan to incorporate ESP32 modules for server communication, providing robust connectivity and enabling seamless data exchange between the system components and our server infrastructure.

Circuit Board Enhancement

For the circuit board, we opted for a general-purpose dotted circuit board. This choice provides the flexibility to customize the circuit board layout as needed. Unlike pre-designed circuit boards, a dotted circuit board allows for easy adjustments and modifications, ensuring that we can tailor the design to meet our evolving needs and integrate new components effectively.

Data Display and Implementation

In the final stage of our project, we will focus on displaying the collected data, including the amount of electricity saved, the reduction in costs, and the overall impact of the project. This information will be showcased on a dedicated webpage hosted on a personal server. The webpage will provide real-time insights into the system’s performance, offering users a clear view of the benefits achieved through our energy-saving measures. By presenting this data, we aim to demonstrate the effectiveness of our system and its potential for broader application.
